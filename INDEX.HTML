1.EMPEROR PENGUIN





The emperor penguin is the tallest and heaviest of all living penguin species and is endemic to Antarctica.

The male and female are similar in plumage and size, reaching 100 cm in length and weighing from 22 to 45 kg. Height: 1.1 – 1.3 m (Adult)

Conservation status: Near Threatened (Population stable)

Scientific name: Aptenodytes forsteri

Lifespan: 20 years

Clutch size: 1

Mass: Male: 23 kg (Adult, After the breeding season)

Female: 23 kg (Adult, After the breeding season)



2.THE ROCK HOPPER PENGUIN





The rockhopper penguins are three closely related taxa of crested penguins that have been traditionally treated as a single species and are sometimes split into three species. Not all experts agree on the classification of these penguins



3.THE KING PENGUIN.





The king penguin is the second largest species of penguin, smaller, but somewhat similar in appearance to the emperor penguin. There are two subspecies: A. p. patagonicus and A. p. halli; patagonicus is found in the South Atlantic and halli in the South Indian Ocean and at Macquarie Island.

Mass: 9.3 – 17 kg

Scientific name: Aptenodytes patagonicus

Conservation status: Least Concern (Population increasing)

Class: Aves

Higher classification: Great penguins

Length: 85 – 95 cm Encyclopedia of Life



4.THE Adélie penguin.




The Adélie penguin is a species of penguin common along the entire coast of the Antarctic continent, which is its only habitat. It is the most widely spread penguin species, as well as the most southerly distributed of all penguins, along with the emperor penguin.

Scientific name: Pygoscelis adeliae

Conservation status: Near Threatened (Population increasing)

Mass: 4.7 kg

Order: Sphenisciformes

Length: 70 cm

Family: Spheniscidae



5.CHINSTRAP PENGUIN.



The chinstrap penguin is a species of penguin that inhabits a variety of islands and shores in the Southern Pacific and the Antarctic Oceans. Its name stems from the narrow black band under its head, which makes it appear as if it were wearing a black helmet, making it easy to identify.

Scientific name: Pygoscelis antarcticus

Lifespan: 15 – 20 years (In the wild)

Mass: 3 – 5 kg

Conservation status: Least Concern (Population increasing)

Higher classification: Brush-tailed penguins

Kingdom: Animalia

6.GENTOO PENGUIN.



The gentoo penguin is a penguin species in the genus Pygoscelis, most closely related to the Adélie penguin and the chinstrap penguin. The earliest scientific description was made in 1781 by Johann Reinhold Forster with a type locality in the Falkland Islands. Mass: 5.9 kg

Conservation status: Near Threatened (Population decreasing)

Scientific name: Pygoscelis papua

Class: Aves

Order: Sphenisciformes

Length: 70 – 95 cm Encyclopedia of Life



7.CRESTED PENGUIN.



The term crested penguin is the common name given collectively to species of penguins of the genus Eudyptes. The exact number of species in the genus varies between four and seven depending on the authority, and a Chatham Islands species became extinct in recent centuries.

Height: Snares penguin: 50 – 70 cm

Scientific name: Eudyptes

Clutch size: Snares penguin: 2

Phylum: Chordata

Mass: Macaroni penguin: 4.7 kg

Fiordland penguin: 3.7 kg

Length: Royal penguin: 70 cm

Southern rockhopper penguin: 55 cm

Snares penguin: 50 – 60 cm
